# LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas)

## 0 What Is LNG
LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas) is natural gas produced from gas fields, primarily methane, that is purified to remove valuable components (such as helium), heavier hydrocarbons, and impurities harmful to downstream use (such as sulfur, nitrogen, and water). The gas is then liquefied through a cryogenic process and becomes a liquid at atmospheric pressure. In general, LNG is liquefied at about -162°C under 1 atm.<sup>[[1]](#ref1)</sup>

## 1 Physicochemical Properties
LNG is mainly composed of methane CH4 (typically above 90%), with ethane C2H6, nitrogen N<sub>2</sub> (about 0.5% to 1%), and trace C<sub>3</sub> to C<sub>5</sub> hydrocarbons. It is colorless, odorless, non-toxic, and non-corrosive. LNG features high calorific value, strong performance, safety, environmental advantages, and relatively low explosion risk. Its volume is about 1/600 of the same amount of gaseous natural gas.<sup>[[1]](#ref1)</sup>

Typical values include density around 430 to 470 kg/m³ (depending on composition), ignition temperature around 650°C, and heating value around 52 MMBtu/t (1 MMBtu = 2.52 x 10^8 cal). The gaseous density is around 1.5 kg/m³, and the flammability range is about 5% to 15% by volume.

In many scenarios, LNG has practical advantages compared with other energy forms:

- High storage efficiency: smaller land footprint and lower investment; 10 m<sup>3</sup> of LNG storage can supply daily gas demand for about 10,000 households.
- High safety: after vaporization, natural gas is much lighter than air and disperses quickly after leakage, reducing accumulation risk.
- Convenient transportation: due to a high liquid/gas density ratio, LNG shrinks volume significantly; roughly 1 volume of LNG can convert to about 624 volumes of gas.
- Environmental protection: LNG has cleaner composition and more complete combustion; emissions of CO2 and NOx are significantly lower than coal, and pollution impact is much lower than LPG and coal.
- Strong social and economic benefits: as a cleaner transition fuel, natural gas can achieve thermal efficiency up to about 55% in power generation, higher than many oil- and coal-based cases; as a transport fuel substitute, LNG can reduce HC, NOx, CO2, and SOx emissions.

## 2 Applications
LNG is widely recognized as one of the cleanest fossil energy sources, with very low sulfur content. For gas-fired power generation, NO<sub>x</sub> and CO<sub>2</sub> emissions are substantially lower than coal-fired power plants.

LNG is mainly used to convert natural gas into liquid form for marine and land transportation, especially where pipeline construction is technically or economically infeasible.<sup>[[2]](#ref2)</sup> Compared with compressed natural gas (CNG), LNG has much higher volumetric energy density; for the same volume, LNG energy density is about 2.4 times that of CNG at 250 bar, or around 60% of diesel fuel.<sup>[[3]](#ref3)</sup>

LNG is transported by dedicated LNG carriers and cryogenic tank trucks, then regasified in destination markets for end use.

Key uses at onshore LNG terminals include:

1. Peak shaving and emergency backup for city gas networks.
2. Main gas source for pipeline supply in medium and large cities.
3. Gas source for LNG communities and industrial users.
4. Fuel for transportation such as vehicles, vessels, and potentially aviation applications.
5. Utilization of LNG cold energy.
6. Distributed energy systems.

## 3 Standards and Quality Requirements
China's national natural gas standard is GB17820-2018. LNG is a different physical state of natural gas, so no separate standalone LNG composition standard is defined in this context; typical density range is 0.42 to 0.46 g/cm3.<sup>[[4]](#ref4)</sup> The specification includes corresponding quality requirements.
