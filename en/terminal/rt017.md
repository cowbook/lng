## Overview

Qingdao LNG was Sinopec's first LNG project. On 14 November 2014, the first LNG carrier, Coronis, arrived and the terminal formally entered operation.

On 10 January 2022, two new send-out routes, the Kunlun Shihua BOG line and the Qingning pipeline, were commissioned together, marking the full operation of the terminal's phase III project and creating an integrated outbound system with two BOG routes and three trunklines. On 2 November 2023, the terminal commissioned the world's largest LNG storage tank and China's first 270,000 m3 LNG tank. On 2 June 2024, it used its dual-berth jetty configuration to complete China's first simultaneous LNG vessel gassing-up trial and cargo discharge operation, opening a new operating model for LNG storage and logistics assets. In 2023, Qingdao LNG ranked third among Chinese LNG terminals by total discharge volume.

### Milestones

#### 14 November 2014

Qingdao LNG discharged its first cargo from Papua New Guinea, marking the commercial start-up of Sinopec's first LNG terminal.

#### 15 April 2015

Asia's first light-hydrocarbon recovery unit at an LNG terminal was completed.

#### 2 March 2018

Cumulative discharge volume exceeded 10 million tonnes.

#### 15 January 2021

A new intelligent operations management platform entered service.

#### 7 September 2022

An intelligent inspection robot was introduced for the first time.

#### 2 November 2023

China's first 270,000 m3 LNG tank, also the world's largest gas storage tank by capacity, entered operation.

#### 29 December 2023

China's first fully automated LNG truck-loading skid was commissioned.

#### 13 January 2024

Shandong's first dual-berth LNG jetty entered operation.

#### 2 June 2024

The terminal completed China's first simultaneous LNG vessel gassing-up trial and unloading operation using its dual-berth jetty.
